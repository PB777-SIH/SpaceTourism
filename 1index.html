History of Rockets and Space Shuttles

Rockets have a rich history dating back to ancient China's invention of gunpowder. From early fireworks to modern space exploration, rockets have propelled humanity beyond Earth's atmosphere. The space shuttle era, beginning in 1981, marked significant milestones like the International Space Station assembly and satellite deployment. These achievements have expanded our understanding of space and paved the way for future exploration.

Humans in Space

Human interactions with space have evolved from early observations to ambitious exploration. Satellites enable global communication, weather forecasting, and navigation. Manned missions, such as the Apollo program, have landed humans on the Moon, while space stations like the ISS facilitate long-duration spaceflight and scientific research. Robotic probes have explored distant planets and asteroids. Private companies are now driving space tourism and commercial satellite launches, opening new frontiers for human presence in space.
